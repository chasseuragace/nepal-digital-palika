# Business Model
## Subscription-Based Digital Infrastructure for Nepalese Local Governments

## From "Buying Software" to "Subscribing to Public Digital Infrastructure"

---

### 1. The Government Procurement Reality

#### What We've Learned from Government Stakeholders

The primary barrier to digital adoption in local governments is **not** technical capability or lack of budget.

It is **procurement risk and cost unpredictability**.

Government officials face:

**❌ Inconsistent Pricing**  
"Why does this vendor charge NPR 500,000 while another charges NPR 2,000,000 for similar features?"

**❌ No Standard Evaluation Framework**  
"How do we justify software value in an audit?"

**❌ Audit Vulnerability**  
"What if we're questioned: 'Why did you pay this much?'"

**❌ One-Time Purchase Problems**  
"We bought the system. Now it's outdated. What do we do?"

**The Result:**
- Arbitrary price caps imposed
- Risk-averse decision making
- Lowest-bid-wins mentality
- Innovation blocked by fear

**👉 Our subscription model directly addresses this fear.**

---

### 2. Core Positioning: Not Software, But Service

#### The Fundamental Shift

> **"We are not selling software licenses.**  
> **We are offering a standardized digital service subscription for local governments."**

This distinction changes everything:

| **Software Purchase Model** | **Digital Service Subscription** |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| One-time payment | Fixed annual or multi-year cost |
| Unclear scope ("what's included?") | Clearly defined feature sets |
| Maintenance as extra cost | Continuous updates included |
| No accountability after handover | Ongoing support & compliance |
| Vendor negotiations each time | Standardized national pricing |
| Becomes obsolete | Always current |

---

### 3. Why Subscription Fits Government Logic

Governments already understand subscriptions for:
- Electricity services
- Telecommunications
- Cloud hosting
- Internet connectivity

**The justification is simple:**

> "We pay **X per year** for **Y defined public digital services**."

**Audit-safe. Repeatable. Defensible.**

#### Benefits for Government Procurement

✓ **Predictability**: Fixed costs over defined periods  
✓ **Transparency**: Clear feature-to-cost mapping  
✓ **Accountability**: Vendor responsible throughout subscription  
✓ **Scalability**: Same model works for 1 Palika or 753  
✓ **Comparability**: Easy to evaluate across vendors  
✓ **Risk Reduction**: No obsolescence, continuous support

### 4. The Tiered Service Model

#### Strategic Insight

**Not all Palikas are tourism destinations.**

Pretending they are weakens the value proposition.

Instead, we offer **modular public digital infrastructure** that serves different Palika realities.

---

### 5. Service Bundle A: Tourism-Focused Palikas

#### **Tourism Portal Bundle**

**Target Palikas:**
- Have heritage sites, monuments, temples
- Host festivals and cultural events
- Experience tourist flow (domestic or international)
- Possess cultural assets worth promoting

**Core Features Included:**

**Tourism Content Management**
- Heritage and attraction profiles (unlimited entries)
- Festival and events calendar
- Blog and storytelling sections
- Media galleries (images, videos)

**Discovery & Experience**
- QR-based heritage site discovery
- Map-based asset navigation
- Audio narration (text-to-speech in multiple languages)
- Multilingual content support (Nepali + international languages)

**Official Presence**
- Responsive tourism website (custom domain)
- Progressive Web App (functions as mobile app)
- Local contact and personnel profiles
- Accommodation and service listings

**Technical Features**
- Content Management System (no technical skills required)
- Media management and optimization
- Mobile-first responsive design
- Offline-capable PWA

**Included Services**
- Hosting and infrastructure
- SSL certificates and security
- Regular system updates
- Content backup and recovery
- Technical support (helpdesk)
- Training for content managers

**Value Logic:**

> "Supports tourism income, increases destination visibility, directly aligns with Tourism Decade goals, empowers local economy."

This architectural decision creates enormous value:

**For Government:**
- One national procurement standard
- One maintenance framework
- One security and compliance model
- One training methodology

**For Palikas:**
- Easy upgrade path between bundles
- No system migration needed
- Preserved content and investment
- Continuous feature additions benefit all

**For the Nation:**
- Unified data standards
- Interoperable systems
- National aggregation capability
- Consistent user experience

**Scenario Example:**

**Year 1**: A rural Palika subscribes to the **Digital Services Bundle**
- Serves citizens, promotes local businesses, provides emergency info

**Year 3**: New heritage site discovered, tourism interest grows
- Palika upgrades to **Tourism Bundle**
- All existing content preserved
- No system rebuild required
- Tourism features activated seamlessly

### 8. Pricing Standardization Philosophy

#### Why Standardization Matters

Instead of negotiating project-by-project, we establish:

> **"Nationally standardized subscription tiers with transparent feature mapping."**

**Example Structure** (Conceptual framework, not actual pricing):

| **Service Tier** | **Target** | **Feature Scope** | **Duration** |
|------------------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Basic Palika Bundle | Citizen services, local governance | Core features | 3-5 years |
| Tourism Bundle | Tourism promotion + culture | Enhanced features | 3-5 years |
| Premium Bundle | Analytics, integrations, custom | Advanced features | 3-5 years |

**Each Tier Includes:**
- Fixed, documented feature set
- All technical infrastructure
- Continuous support and updates
- Training and capacity building
- Security and compliance

**Government Benefits:**
- No arbitrary pricing
- Easy audit justification
- Fair treatment across Palikas
- Scalable procurement framework

### 16. Addressing Common Objections

#### **Objection 1**: "Why not build our own system?"

**Response**: Building once costs more, maintaining forever costs exponentially more. Subscription ensures the system improves continuously without additional Palika investment. Government focuses on governance, we focus on technology.

---

#### **Objection 2**: "What if we want to stop after one year?"

**Response**: Subscription terms are 3-5 years for cost efficiency, but include clear exit terms. However, the value compounds over time—year 3 is better than year 1 because content, analytics, and community benefits accumulate.

---

#### **Objection 3**: "Other vendors offer cheaper one-time builds."

**Response**: True initial cost may be lower. But ask: What's included? Who maintains it? Who updates it when security vulnerabilities emerge? What happens when mobile OS updates break the app? The real cost is lifetime cost, not initial cost.

---

#### **Objection 4**: "Can't we negotiate different features?"

**Response**: Standardization is a feature, not a limitation. It ensures fairness, comparability, and procurement safety. Custom projects create custom risks. However, we welcome feedback to improve standard tiers for all Palikas.
