# Executive Summary
## National Digital Tourism Infrastructure for Local Governments of Nepal

---

### The Opportunity

Nepal's tourism sector stands at a critical juncture. While the **Nepal Tourism Decade (2023–2032)** sets ambitious goals for sustainable and inclusive tourism growth, local governments face a fundamental challenge: **their tourism assets remain digitally invisible, fragmented, and difficult to discover**.

This is not a technology problem. It is a **governance infrastructure problem**.

---

### The Core Challenge

**What Government Faces Today:**

- **Fragmented Information**: Tourism content scattered across PDFs, social media, and informal sources with no official standard
- **Local Invisibility**: Rich cultural assets, festivals, heritages, and trails remain undocumented at the Palika level
- **Weak Digital Capacity**: Most Palikas lack dedicated tourism portals, content management systems, or technical resources
- **Repetitive Costs**: Each Palika separately contracts vendors for websites, apps, QR systems—with no reuse or national coordination
- **Procurement Risk**: One-time software purchases create audit concerns, unclear maintenance costs, and rapid obsolescence

**The Result**: Despite strong policy intent, local tourism assets remain underrepresented, while governments struggle with unpredictable costs and vendor lock-in.

---

### What We Propose

**A standardized, subscription-based digital service platform** that enables every Palika to:

✓ Manage official tourism and governance content  
✓ Present heritage, festivals, and local assets professionally  
✓ Serve citizens with emergency information and local services  
✓ Connect physical sites to digital content via QR codes  
✓ Operate in multiple languages with accessibility features  

**Delivered through:**
- Responsive tourism portals
- Progressive Web Apps (mobile-ready, no app store needed)
- QR-enabled heritage discovery
- Unified national backend with local ownership

---

### The Strategic Difference

#### **We Are Not Selling Software**

> "We offer standardized digital infrastructure as a service—like utilities, not like assets."

**Traditional Approach:**
- One-time purchase of "tourism software"
- Unclear maintenance, upgrades, support scope
- Each Palika negotiates separately
- High audit risk: "Why did we pay this much?"

**Our Approach:**
- **Fixed-term subscription** (3–5 years)
- **Clearly defined service tiers** (Tourism Bundle / Palika Services Bundle)
- **Transparent, standardized pricing** across all Palikas
- **Continuous updates, support, and compliance** included
- **Low procurement risk**: Predictable, auditable, repeatable

---

### Two Service Bundles, One Platform

#### **Tourism-Focused Bundle**
*For Palikas with heritage sites, festivals, and tourist flow*

Includes:
- Tourism website & Progressive Web App
- Heritage and attraction profiles
- QR-based site discovery
- Multilingual content & text-to-speech
- Events and festivals calendar
- Media galleries and storytelling
- Local contact and personnel profiles

**Value**: Drives tourism visibility, supports local economy, aligns with national tourism goals

---

#### **Palika Digital Services Bundle**
*For non-tourism or emerging Palikas*

Includes:
- Official Palika portal
- Emergency (SOS) information system
- Local marketplace (producers, services)
- Public notification board
- Entity profiling (schools, hospitals, offices)
- Map-based service discovery

**Value**: Improves citizen services, strengthens local governance, builds digital readiness

---

### The Critical Insight

> **Both bundles run on the same underlying platform.**

This means:
- One national standard
- One maintenance model
- One pricing framework
- Easy upgrade path as Palikas develop

**Today**: A Palika chooses the services bundle  
**Tomorrow**: As tourism assets emerge, they upgrade to the tourism bundle—without rebuilding
